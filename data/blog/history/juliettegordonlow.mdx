* American activist and founder of the Girl Scouts USA [^1] with the help of Robert Baden, founder of the Scout movement (1860 - 1927).
* Born in Savannah Georgia, Gordon suffered as a child from constant accidents and illnesses that weakened her hearing ability. 
* He used her free time to develop her artistic skills, with her cousins ​​he started a local newspaper called *The Malbone Bouquet*.
* After the death of her husband William Mackay Low, Gordon started traveling and in 1911 she meets Robert Baden.
* Inspired by the Boys Scouts that had 40,000 members at the time, she decides to form an analogous group called the Girl Guides. 
* The Scout movement was intended to teach children survival skills and military discipline in the case of a foreign invasion.
* Gordon invested all of her money and time in promoting the group, then she wrote the book *How Girls Can Help Their Country*.
* The book was based on *Scouting for Boys* by Robert and *How Girls Can Help Build Up The Empire* by Agnes.
* To make the project official, she changed the name of Girl Guides to Girl Scouts and by 1915 it had 73 sponsors and 2,400 members. 
* Gordon continued promoting the institution internationally until her death from breast cancer at the age of 66.

[^1]: https://www.girlscouts.org/
